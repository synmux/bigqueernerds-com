# 🏳️‍🌈 BIG QUEER NERDS: a manifesto

The tech industry runs on queer and neurodivergent labour. It always has. We built the protocols, wrote the compilers, and kept the servers breathing at 3am — and in exchange we've been underpaid, burnt out, talked over, and asked to tone it down for the client call.

Every consultancy you've ever worked for exists to do one thing: widen the gap between what the client pays and what the worker costs, then hand the difference to people who did neither the work nor the paying.

**BIG QUEER NERDS** is the opposite of that. On purpose. In writing.

The name is a purposeful decision. If it turns a client off, we probably didn't want that client anyway.

## What we believe

- **Queer and neurodivergent people are talent-rich and chronically underpaid.** We intend to correct this, starting with ourselves.
- **A company is its people.** Not its brand, not its cap table, not its founder's LinkedIn.
- **Profit is fuel, not destination.** We make money so the people here can live well. We do not exist so the money can.
- **Growth is a choice, not a commandment.** We expand when it makes life better for the people already here — and not one hire sooner.

## What we promise

1. **Accountable to workers, not shareholders.** There are no shareholders to appease. The people who do the work make the decisions, with radical transparency about how and why.

2. **When we win, everyone wins.** Profits are shared among all of us — weighted by seniority, because we're radical, not naive — but reaching _everyone_, down to the newest and least glamorous role. When the company does better, so does every single person in it.

3. **A floor below which nobody falls.** A dependable base salary you can plan a life around, plus a minimum floor beneath which no role pays. When the floor rises, it rises for everyone stood on it — and workers help decide when.

4. **Unlimited sick leave. Actually unlimited.** No doctor's-note theatre, no wellness quotas, no questions. Bodies and brains break down; a company that punishes that is a company that eats its people.

5. **Time off without interrogation.** Up to a generous threshold: no questions asked. Beyond it: questions asked kindly, and answered with "yes" wherever humanly possible.

6. **Engineered for brains that don't run corporate firmware.** We staff every job with more people than strictly necessary, because redundancy isn't waste — ask any SRE. When someone's brain checks out for a day (and someone's will; that's the whole point), the work doesn't collapse and neither do they. n+1, but for humans.

7. **Remote-first, permanently.** No office, no commute, and an allowance to build a workspace that actually works for you. Meet-ups once or twice a year, genuinely optional — where "optional" means _optional_, not "career-limiting to skip".

8. **Nobody faces a hostile client alone.** Client-facing work is an opt-in rota, never an obligation. And if a customer treats our people badly, we can vote to fire the customer. We will turn down money before we spend our people on it.

9. **Protection with teeth.** Anti-discrimination protection that covers how clients treat us, not just how we treat each other. Being paid to be there does not mean being paid to take it.

10. **Compassion before suspicion.** If someone's struggling, we assume struggle — not bad faith. Safeguards against genuine bad actors exist, and are used only when we're certain that's what we're looking at.

## What we are not

- **Not a charity.** We charge properly, deliver properly, and are extremely good at what we do. Treating workers well isn't a discount — it's why the work is better.
- **Not a startup chasing an exit.** There is no exit. The company is the point.
- **Not a family.** Families don't pay you. We're colleagues who have your back, which is considerably more useful.
- **Not a culture fit.** We _are_ the misfits. Fit accordingly.

## What we do

The unglamorous, load-bearing work: infrastructure, cloud, networking, development, and the support that keeps businesses actually running. The stuff that pages someone at 3am — done by people rested, paid, and respected enough to care that it doesn't.

## The honest bit

We're at the beginning. Early salaries will be modest, and early members take on more risk — in exchange for a bigger share of what we build and a genuine say in how we build it. We won't promise what we can't fund; we'd rather promise carefully and over-deliver than post inspirational values we quietly walk back.

And yes — if the word "co-operative" just lit up in your head, you're right, and we're doing the homework.

This document will change as we do. That's rather the point.
